In 2009, Hammett was ranked number 15 in Joel McIver's book The 100 Greatest Metal Guitarists.

Kirk Lee Hammett Kirk Lee Hammett (born November 18, 1962)[1] is an American musician who has been the lead guitarist and a contributing songwriter for heavy metal band Metallica since 1983.
Before joining Metallica, he formed and named the band Exodus. In 2003, Hammett was ranked 11th on Rolling Stone's list of The 100 Greatest Guitarists of All Time.
